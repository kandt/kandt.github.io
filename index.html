Our Story

(which may or may not be different)

Kumneger
Her story

Kumneger

I am sure Temesghen will tell you a (very) different story about how we met but here is the truth: he pretty much stalked me! Oookay, it wasn't quite like that...it was, however, coincidental that we would meet in the ONLY Ethiopian/Eritrean restaurant in IOWA CITY (and probably the entire State of Iowa).

Temesghen may tell you that it was because I was the only habesha person he knew in Iowa City (or that I had cable) that he hung on to me so tightly but lets be honest, it was because he needed someone to help him transition from the Queen's English (and sometimes, Italian words where he drops the last vowel because he thinks it becomes an English word) to mid-Western American English. Who says, 'quid' and 'knackered' anyway?? And because dropping the 'e' in 'importante' makes it an English word, it does not work for all other Italian words.

A lot of our time is spent with me helping him assimilate to American life, him teaching me (more like him talking and me violently nodding my head in the hopes I don't get asked a question) programming languages and the many tools he develops, and more importantly learning and navigating adulthood together.

We are beyond blessed to have family and friends that regularly demonstrate the meaning of love and marriage. I am excited to marry this man who is loving, caring, goofy, smart, thoughtful, and funny!!

Temesghen
His story

Temesghen

Depending on who you ask, you may get two different stories about how we first met. We met in Iowa City. I was fresh out of the Welsh boat and Kumneger was a distressed law student. Who made the first move, you ask? Of course she did. After all, what do you expect when a handsome chap lands in the land of the corn fields? No time has been wasted by Kumneger Emiru, she took this once in a life time opportunity and she ran with it.

All jokes aside, the lovely woman on the other side is truly a special one. Words can't express how truly blessed we are to have found each other. I can't imagine a journey to Mars without her (because we are going to Mars, damn it!!!).
